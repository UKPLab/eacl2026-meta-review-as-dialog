Decision Maker: I am trying to understand the situation in Afghanistan and Pakistan, and whether America can succeed there or not. Can you provide me with some insights and arguments?

Dialogue Agent: Of course, the situation in Afghanistan and Pakistan is complex and challenging. However, it is possible for America to succeed in the region. The question is what success means and how we can achieve it.

Decision Maker: What do you mean by success?

Dialogue Agent: Success could be defined as contributing to the persistence of an Afghan government that is not forcibly ruled by the Taliban and a Pakistan that does not completely fail as a state or fall into the hands of Islamic extremists. It is a minimal standard, but the consequences of failure are potentially catastrophic.

Decision Maker: What are the arguments against America's success in the region?

Dialogue Agent: Some argue that the task is too big for America, and the application of counterinsurgency, which is a strategy of political reform, economic development, and counter-guerilla operations, is difficult and impractical in Afghanistan. Others believe that the lack of clarity of objectives and game plan in American policy, corruption, and weak leadership in Afghanistan and Pakistan make success impossible.

Decision Maker: What are the arguments that support America's success?

Dialogue Agent: First, we can succeed because there is still time to define our goals and allocate our resources more realistically. Second, the situation in the region is not as dire as many believe. Contrary to popular belief, Afghanistan was a coherent and mainly peaceful independent state before the wars ignited by the Soviet invasion of 1979. Despite the manifold errors of US policy during the Bush administration, a strong plurality of Afghans still want to reclaim their peaceful decentralized, but nonetheless unified and even modernizing state. Millions of Afghans risked their lives to vote in the recent Presidential election, and a strong majority of Afghans still have a more favorable view of international forces in their country than they do of the Taliban.

Decision Maker: How can America achieve success in the region?

Dialogue Agent: America can achieve success by conducting combat operations against its enemies, training and equipping host nation security forces, providing essential services to the population, providing them with good governance and economic development, and by peeling away the less committed parts of the insurgency. The single most important step is to raise and train an Afghan army that is sufficiently sized and sufficiently well-equipped to secure Afghanistan on its own.
