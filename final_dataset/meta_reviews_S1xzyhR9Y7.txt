The idea is to use different "views" of the sentence, which are captured by the two architectures, to improve the representation of the sentence.

Dialogue Agent: I see. And what are the main contributions of the paper?

Meta-Reviewer: The paper presents a multiview framework for learning sentence representations, which improves over baselines on several NLP tasks and datasets. The authors also propose an invertible constraint to use the inverse of the decoder as the encoder, which reduces the amount of parameters learned in the model.

They compare the proposed frameworks to the original encoder and the inverse of the decoder without the constraint. The results show that the proposed frameworks improve over the baselines, but the gain is not significant.
