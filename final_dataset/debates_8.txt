Decision Maker: What about the argument that gun bans do not work to reduce crime?

Dialogue Agent: The argument for gun bans is that it would reduce the number of guns in circulation and therefore reduce the number of crimes committed with guns. They point to examples such as the D.C. handgun ban in 1976, which led to an increase in crime rates compared to neighboring states and the United States as a whole. Dialogue Agent: The argument against an increase in armed citizens is that it could increase the amount of gun theft and lead to criminals arming themselves as well. Additionally, it is not clear how many of the gun owners with concealed carry permits are actually carrying their guns at any given time, leaving them vulnerable to potential attacks. The argument for gun ownership is that citizens should have the freedom to choose to protect themselves if they wish to do so and not be subject to restrictions on their constitutional rights.
